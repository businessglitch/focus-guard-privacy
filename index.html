TL;DR: Focus Guard does not collect, store, or share any of your personal data. Everything stays on your device.

Data Collection

Focus Guard does not collect any user data. We do not track, store, or transmit any information about you or your browsing habits.

Local Storage

Focus Guard stores your preferences (such as blocked websites list, blocking duration, and settings) locally on your device using Chrome's built-in storage system. This data:

  • Remains on your device only
  • Is never transmitted to external servers
  • Is not accessible to other websites or extensions
  • Can be cleared by uninstalling the extension

Permissions

Focus Guard requires certain permissions to function:

  • Host permissions: To check if visited websites are in your blocked list
  • Storage: To save your settings locally on your device
  • Tabs: To redirect blocked websites to the blocking page
  • Notifications: To inform you when blocking periods end

These permissions are used solely for the extension's core functionality and not for data collection.

Third Parties

Focus Guard does not share any information with third parties because we don't collect any information in the first place.

Analytics

Focus Guard does not use any analytics, tracking pixels, or monitoring tools.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever change our privacy practices (which would only happen if we added new features), we will update this policy and notify users through the Chrome Web Store.

ADHD and Neurodiversity

Focus Guard is designed with ADHD brains in mind, providing gentle, understanding reminders rather than harsh blocking. We respect the unique challenges of neurodivergent individuals and never judge or shame users for needing focus support.
